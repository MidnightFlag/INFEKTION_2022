Crystal Software Announces TextPipe 5.1

Crystal Software Corporation has announced TextPipe 5.1, an update of its text cleansing and transformation workbench for data warehousing, website maintenance, and electronic text processing. TextPipe 5.1 solves common data cleansing problems with exact matching, pattern matching, sounds-like matching, and new edit distance matching to cleanse typographical errors. Edit distance matching replaces a target word when the number of character swaps, inserts, or deletes from a selected word is below a chosen threshold.

TextPipe 5.1 automates complex text changes and reformatting across entire websites, source code files, and text or binary files up to 2GB. New shallow XML parsing manipulates XML files larger than available memory and allows search and replace in text subsets like line ranges, column ranges, CSV fields, tab-delimited fields, lines matching a pattern, and search results.

TextPipe 5.1 costs $70 for a single-user license. A free evaluation copy is available from the company's website.

Asynchrony.com Launches Beta Testing Facility

Asynchrony.com, Inc. has launched a beta testing facility that allows anyone with Internet access to share in the profits from software they download and help test. Asynchrony.com allows software developers from around the globe to meet each other, initiate software projects, and form teams that will share in the revenues of the products they create. Through the beta testing facility, non-programmers who contribute to projects by finding and reporting problems or bugs are eligible to receive shares in the future revenues of products.

Compensating beta testers is an extension of the company's philosophy that those who do the work of creating software should share the profits. MySQL AB and Sleepycat Software Unveil MaxSQL

MySQL AB and Sleepycat Software, Inc. have introduced MaxSQL, a new Open Source, high-performance, and fully transactional SQL server. MaxSQL, developed jointly by the two companies, combines the industry-standard interface of MySQL's data access language with the high-performance transaction services of Sleepycat's Berkeley DB.

MaxSQL provides the ability to perform transactions with full recoverability for committed changes. It manages databases up to 256 terabytes in size, accommodates many concurrent users, and survives power failures or system crashes without losing data.

MaxSQL may be downloaded free of charge from www.maxsql.com.
